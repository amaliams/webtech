Client: Hellmist Dragonfire
Website name: hellmistdragonfire.com

Purpose, goals and audience

The purpose of the website is to create a closer link between the band and their fans. To intrigue new followers and create a lager fan base. The web site will also supply fans with music and products to increase the Hellmist Dragonfire fever that it gripping the world. The site also supply the fans with information about that they have and are doing in terms of day to day and big tours. Our audience is new fans and old fans; people how have been to a concert or just heard a song they liked and want to get to know the band. Agents will also find contact information and a first impression if they are looking to book the band for different giggs.

Demographics

Our target demographics is the fans of Hellmist Dragonfire. The band is a older band that was at its peak in the 70-80 therefor the average user is a somewhat older man he has a blue collar job and liks drinking and hanging out with the guys.

Navigation structure

We have chosen to use the clique navigation structure(image shown below) for our client’s website. This allows the users to navigate throughout the page with much greater freedom than hierarchical structure. Users can go from one page to another by using the navigation bar on top of the window, and the navigation bar re-appears on every sub-sites giving users the ability switch site anytime they want. On the left end of the navigation bar is where our client’s logo sits which also acts as a button to return to home page.

There are mainly two reasons for why we think this navigation structure is best for this website. First, as mentioned above, it gives the users more freedom of navigation, thus omitting the chance of users getting confused or bored. Second, this structure is also comparatively intuitive, being used by many websites worldwide, which encourage the users to go to all sites in the domain.

This structure is particularly useful because it’s simple to navigate throughout the various elements of the website. Ideally the band wants the fans to be able to easily jump from one section to any other and this has a higher priority than having a very stylized website. The structure is very intuitive, as stated earlier and will not be confusing to fans of any ages.



Page Layout and Appearance

For the universal design we only use a thin navigation bar and a footer. Our main priority is the band and giving the visitors a good impression of them. therefor we only use a very simple navigation bar with a clean look that lets you know where you are on the site and how to move wherever you want in a single step. The band is in focus and we have designed the navigation bar and footer to take the least amount an attention away from the content of the page while still being easy to use. We feel at a simple to use web page with a dark color scheme fits well with the band’s graphic design and logos, and will appeal to our demographic while helping them navigate with there limited technical skills.

Content

Home page - main.html

When users first visit the website they will be greeted our home page. Our home page must therefore create a good first impression to attract and keep the users engaged on our website. With that being said, we will include a large slideshow of images which covers the whole window to capture the users’ attention as well as keep them up to date with the latest events.

When scrolled down, the users will encounter a short news section where they could find more information about the latest events and announcement. Each of the news segment will have its own picture to the left, and the text should be short and concise. The goal is to give the users an intuitive and simplistic UI.

The navigation bar should have a height of 90px, while the footer’s height will depend on the content we choose to include, for instance, contact information, our clients logo, address, Facebook, YouTube and Twitter. Footer will act a portal for links outside of our website.




Shop - shop.html

In this section the shop section the first thing the user sees is a rotating gallery of different things and special offers they can buy. We have chosen to divide the page into three different parts one for music one for different merchandise called items and one called tickets as seen in the picture above. When you press any one of the tickets, items or music buttons above you immediately change the content of the page underneath. For example if the ticket button is pressed all the different concerts comes to view. You then simply press the picture with the image and concert name on it to see all the relevant details and make a purchase.

The rotating pictures is the first thing the user sees and will show of the best offers available. this will also help the user see what is new from the last time he or she visited the page. Because the shop section contains a lot of information we have chosen til divide it into different sub sections. this is to avoid presenting the user with to much information at once and risking they be put off. When the different tabs are opened we have organised the information in a manner that makes it easy to see what is available and you can check out the different offers while staying on the same page the relevant information simply becomes visible or invisible by the press of a button.




About - about.html

This about page will provide information of the band’s history and its member in detail. On top and first on the page is the pictures of every member of the band. Clicking on a picture will send to the corresponding biography of the member. Further down is the history of the band, which goes through the early days of the band and how they started up to the bands position today. A timeline might be included (depends on workload) to further enhance the experience. Below the history of the band, comes a short biography of each of the members. Each of the biography should be short and concise to go along with the theme of the website.

The goal of the page is of course to give users an introduction to Hellmist Dragonfire. The website will use extensive amount of pictures to tell the story of the band. The layout of the page is meant to give the users a tour through the band’s history, the timeline will also make it easier for the users to navigate it.




Tour - tour.html

The band wants to promote there concerts tours and other events to ensure that the most amount of people attend and hype is created. therefor we have made a simple navigation bar just for this page to ensure that users find what they are looking for fast. there is also a big picture to set the mood on the page and get you ready for some fun. Underneath there is a text describing different aspects of the tour/event and talking about why you should take the trip to come and see them. As an extra cool feature the map is interactive so you can press on your town at see the relevant information about that specific event and place an order.

We believe that information is key in bringing people to the events. and that our older fans will find the information regarding why the tour or concert is held to be interesting. This page will be important as we believe that there is a fair percentile of our target demographic that is not active on social media, and will use this as their main source of information.

Blog - blog.html

This page is meant to give the bands fans a more personal and less serious way of following them. this is a simple page that chronologically show what the band has been up to. every day the band and the team post on the blog talking about serious and less serious things related to the band. readers get to participate by liking posts and been able to add comments on specific entries in the blog.

The thought is that fans will develop a greater loyalty to the band if they get to see and almost join in on the ups and downs of the band. Minimum Requirements

Although the requirement only specify 4 distinct javascript implementations, we have included more than that as a challenge for ourselves, however, we might consider dropping some if the workload turns out beyond our expectations. With that being said, here is a list of our javascript implementations.

  • buybutton.js
    This script will aid the users of this website to further their purchase. When clicked, a small window will appear with forms for the users to type in their debit/credit card information, which allows our client to gather necessary information to make the transactions. (The back end of this function is not in our scope.)

  • gallery.js
    A javascript function that lines the images in gallery.html to the bottom of the page then displays them to the screen when chosen. The image-bar will take up 10% of the height of the screen and spans the whole width of the window. Additionally, as more images are added to the gallery, the images will stack on top of each other leaving the left part of the image exposed, so when hovering with the mouse over the image will result in the image move to front to give the user a small preview.

  • slideshow.js
    A function that switches the images in main.html over a certain amount of time (5 sec). And after the last image the slideshow switches back to the first image so cycle begin again in a loop. We want the images to reflect the state of the band by showing the latest festival or concert.

  • comment.js
    A function that allows the users to comment to the band blog posts as well as read the other fans’ comment.

  • play-music.js
    This function gives the users a chance to listen to some of our client’s music. We will include a play, a pause and a stop button, also on the left side of these buttons is an animation indicating that the music is playing. Our client want the users to be able to try the music before actually buying it.

  • map.js
    This javascript function, similar to the buy button, will open a new tiny window to show informations about the various city in a tour, what time and date and where it takes place. The users will also be able to click on a link to transfer them to shop.html to purchase tickets for the concerts.

  • timeline.js
    In about.html there would be a timeline which when clicked leads the users to the according events in the band’s history. This is to create a cleaner and more intuitive experience for the users.
